Ferrari 250

The first 250 was the experimental 250 S berlinetta prototype entered in the 1952 Mille Miglia for Giovanni Bracco and Alfonso Rolfo.

For more information, visit the Ferrari 250 Wikipedia page.

Ferrari 250 MM

Following the success of the 250 S in the Mille Miglia, Ferrari showed a more conventional chassis for the new 250 engine at the 1952 Paris Motor Show.

Ferrari 250 Monza

The 1954 250 Monza was an unusual hybrid of the light four-cylinder 500 Mondial and the 250 line. The model used the 250 MM engine in the short-wheelbase chassis from the 500 Mondial.

Other Ferrari 250 Models

  • Ferrari 250 GT Berlinetta Lusso: Known for its stylish design and luxurious features, it was introduced in 1963 as one of Ferrari's most elegant models.
  • Ferrari 250 GTO: The iconic race car with a powerful V12 engine, produced from 1962 to 1964. It remains one of the most coveted classic cars worldwide.
  • Ferrari 250 GT California Spyder: A beautiful open-top model aimed at the American market, famed for its sporty yet elegant design.
  • Ferrari 250 Testa Rossa: Introduced in 1957, it became a successful racing car, easily recognizable by its unique pontoon fenders.
